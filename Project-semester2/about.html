About Us

Who are IPAA's Members?

Private and corporate membership is extensive and is drawn from Commonwealth and State Government Agencies, Local Government, Government Business Enterprises, Statutory Authorities, Public Bodies and the University. IPAA also has several private sector members, as our organisation represents a key resource for gaining insight into current public sector management issues.

What Does the Institute Provide?

  • professional networking opportunities

  • seminars with key speakers on topical subjects, designed for maximum interaction by members, and directed towards improving expertise in specific techniques and disciplines

  • afternoon Forums with top line panels of speakers on important issues concerning public administration

  • special events

  • awards and prizes for agencies, Councils and GBE's

  • the Australian Journal of Public Administration, the IPAA's authoritative national quarterly publication

  • a three-day National Conference hosted by each Division in turn

    Awards


    National Fellow Award

    To be made a National Fellow is the highest accolade for IPAA members. This prestigious award recognises the recipient's outstanding contribution to the practice and study of public administration.

    Program Workplace Project Awards

    IPAA Tasmania presents a prize to the Public Sector Management Program graduate with the highest mark in the work based project at the annual Public Sector Management Program graduation held at Parliament House.

    Public Sector Excellence Awards

    The Public Sector Excellence Awards recognise outstanding individuals and teams who are leading the way in public administration and making a positive difference for the Tasmanian community.

    Student Awards

    IPAA Tasmania, in conjunction with UTAS School of Social Sciences, award three prizes each year to the highest achievers in policy units at the University of Tasmania.

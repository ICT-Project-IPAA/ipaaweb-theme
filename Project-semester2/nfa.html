National Fellow Award

To be made a National Fellow is the highest accolade for IPAA members. This prestigious award recognises the recipient's outstanding contribution to the practice and study of public administration.

The divisions nominate prospective National Fellows to the National Council, which reviews their application against certain criteria, including the nominee's contribution to the work of IPAA. Winners are announced each year at the IPAA National Conference.

Greg Johannes, Secretary of Department of Premier and Cabinet, is the recipient of 2015 National Fellow Award. Mr Johannes was recognised for his contribution to policy development and implementation in Tasmania. Throughout his career in the public sector he has also led some complex and important policy issues impacting on Tasmania, including areas of climate change, the Tasmania Forestry Agreement, national health reform, social inclusion, cost of living strategies, and family violence. As Secretary of Department of Premier and Cabinet, he is a strong advocate of cross-agency collaboration and has been a key player in the development of relationships between the state government and the University of Tasmania.

During his five year tenure as President of IPAA Tasmania, Mr Johannes led the re-establishment of the association following a significant period of decline, transforming it into a highly valued resource for the public sector in Tasmania.

Student Awards

UTAS Award Winners - 2014 Academic Year

Michelle Richardson, Benjamin Lyall, Lachlan Johnson (from left to right) received their awards from David Nicholson, IPAA Tasmania President and Dr Matt Sussex, Director of Politics and Industrial Relations of University of Tasmania.

On 9 July 2015, IPAA Tasmania was hosted by UTAS at a morning tea to celebrate and present the Student Awards for the 2014 academic year.

The winners are:

  • BJ Thompston Prize for the student obtaining the best results in Public Policy units at level 200 - Lachlan Johnson.

  • IPAA Tasmania Prize awarded annually to the best student in Introduction to Politics and Policy - Benjamin Lyall.

  • IPAA Tasmania Prize for highest mark in a policy unit at any level - Michelle Richardson.

    • IPAA Tasmania, in conjunction with UTAS School of Social Sciences, award three prizes each year to the highest achievers in policy units at the University of Tasmania. Past prize winners are:

    BJ Thompson Prize – for the best results in Public Policy units at 200 level

    Members of the Royal Institute of Public Administration Tasmania (now IPAA Tasmania), officers of the State Public Service, and friends and associates of B.J. Thompson, Esquire (former Public Service Commissioner for Tasmania) wanted to record their appreciation of his work in the field of public administration. They donated a sum to endow a prize in his honour, in public administration. The University Council agreed to the foundation of a prize in 1966.

    IPAA Tasmania Prize – for the highest mark in a policy unit at any level

    IPAA Tasmania Prize – for Introduction to Politics and Policy
